# Lead Generation And Outreach System

This is the operating model for how UtahLister should attract, qualify, and convert early leads.

## Channel Priority Order

### 1. Warm local network for `New Listing Build`

Start with:

- personal referrals
- previous buyers or sellers who trust your judgment
- local friends and family who can refer movers, downsizers, and busy households

This is the fastest path to early proof.

### 2. Partner channels

Prioritize referral relationships with:

- home organizers
- movers
- realtors
- estate cleanout helpers
- local stagers and decluttering professionals

These partners naturally see the exact moment when a seller needs UtahLister.

### 3. Local trust surfaces

Make sure UtahLister appears in the places people check when trust is low:

- `utahlister.com`
- Google Business Profile
- KSL service listing
- Nextdoor or equivalent local neighborhood channels

### 4. Problem-first educational content

Use educational content as a slower-burn acquisition and trust channel.

Best fit:

- Utah-specific search questions
- `KSL vs Facebook Marketplace` comparisons
- pricing and rescue explainers
- category-specific posts for furniture, bikes, tools, outdoor gear, and baby/kids items
- proof-backed case studies

The point is not volume for its own sake.

The point is to capture sellers who already feel the problem UtahLister solves.

### 5. Controlled direct outreach for `Listing Rescue`

Use direct outreach only in narrow, manual cases.

Best use cases:

- founder testing on your own stale listings first
- one-to-one rescue review offers on clearly stale listings
- partner development with organizers, movers, and similar referrers

Do not use:

- bulk seller DMs
- scraping or automated outreach
- fake buyer angles
- repeated outreach after silence

### 6. Proof-driven follow-up

Every strong result should feed:

- the website
- outreach messages
- proof folders
- future customer trust

## The Lead Flow

### New Listing Build flow

1. Lead sees proof, referral, partner mention, or website
2. Lead starts with one-item intake
3. UtahLister qualifies the item and seller fit
4. Lead becomes an active listing job
5. Delivery is completed
6. Proof, outcome notes, and possible review request are captured

### Listing Rescue flow

1. UtahLister identifies or receives a stale listing candidate
2. Seller receives one permission-based rescue offer
3. If interested, seller completes a lightweight rescue screen
4. UtahLister qualifies whether the listing is actually rescuable
5. Seller completes dynamic follow-up intake if needed
6. Rescue package, relist guidance, and follow-up are captured

## Best Messaging Angles

Lead with:

- less work on the seller's side
- better listing clarity
- no password handoff
- seller keeps the account and payout
- Utah-specific platform focus

Do not lead with:

- automation
- AI
- "optimization"
- generic social-media-style hype
- price criticism in the first touch

## Best Entry Offer

The right first CTA depends on the path.

For new work:

`Start with one item.`

For rescue:

`Get one stale listing reviewed first.`

That keeps the decision small and the operational system clean.

## Outreach Rhythm

### Weekly founder cadence

- send 5 to 10 partner outreach touches
- follow up on existing warm leads
- publish or refresh one proof-driven asset
- review which channel produced the highest-quality conversations
- run rescue only as a tiny manual pilot, not a scale channel

### Monthly founder cadence

- ask for reviews from real satisfied customers
- refresh the best proof on the website
- drop weak channels that create noise without conversion

## Lead Quality Rules

Good leads usually have:

- a real item ready to move
- usable photos or a willingness to provide them
- a clear sale goal
- realistic value expectations

Low-quality leads usually show:

- unclear or missing item details
- very low item value
- unlimited support expectations
- refusal to follow the intake structure

## Automation Rule

Do not build or scale outreach automation until:

- the repo docs are stable
- the website copy is finalized
- the content and CTA structure are stable
- the manual rescue rules are proven
- the manual lead stages are clearly defined

Until then, use manual review and local-trust channels to avoid optimizing around a moving target.
