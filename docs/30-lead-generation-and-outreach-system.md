# Lead Generation And Outreach System

This is the operating model for how UtahLister should attract, qualify, and convert early leads.

## Channel Priority Order

### 1. Warm local network

Start with:

- personal referrals
- previous buyers or sellers who trust your judgment
- local friends and family who can refer movers, downsizers, and busy households

This is the fastest path to early proof.

### 2. Partner channels

Prioritize referral relationships with:

- home organizers
- movers
- realtors
- estate cleanout helpers
- local stagers and decluttering professionals

These partners naturally see the exact moment when a seller needs UtahLister.

### 3. Local trust surfaces

Make sure UtahLister appears in the places people check when trust is low:

- `utahlister.com`
- Google Business Profile
- KSL service listing
- Nextdoor or equivalent local neighborhood channels

### 4. Direct outreach

Use direct outreach for partner development, not random mass cold outreach.

Best use cases:

- introducing UtahLister to organizers and movers
- sharing one clean proof asset
- offering a simple one-item starting point for their clients

### 5. Proof-driven follow-up

Every strong result should feed:

- the website
- outreach messages
- proof folders
- future customer trust

## The Lead Flow

1. Stranger sees proof, referral, or website
2. Lead submits one-item intake
3. UtahLister qualifies the item and seller fit
4. Lead becomes an active listing job
5. Delivery gets completed
6. Proof, outcome notes, and possible review request are captured

## Best Messaging Angles

Lead with:

- less work on the seller's side
- better listing clarity
- no password handoff
- seller keeps the account and payout
- Utah-specific platform focus

Do not lead with:

- automation
- AI
- "optimization"
- generic social-media-style hype

## Best Entry Offer

The right first CTA is:

`Start with one item.`

That keeps the buying decision small and the operational system clean.

## Outreach Rhythm

### Weekly founder cadence

- send 5 to 10 partner outreach touches
- follow up on existing warm leads
- publish or refresh one proof-driven asset
- review which channel produced the highest-quality conversations

### Monthly founder cadence

- ask for reviews from real satisfied customers
- refresh the best proof on the website
- drop weak channels that create noise without conversion

## Lead Quality Rules

Good leads usually have:

- a real item ready to move
- usable photos or a willingness to provide them
- a clear sale goal
- realistic value expectations

Low-quality leads usually show:

- unclear or missing item details
- very low item value
- unlimited support expectations
- refusal to follow the intake structure

## Automation Rule

UtahLister already has lead automation in place.

Do not tune that automation until:

- the repo docs are stable
- the website copy is finalized
- the Notion system is built
- the manual lead stages are clearly defined

Until then, use manual review and the current workflow to avoid optimizing around a moving target.
