# Founder Start Here

This is the shortest path through the UtahLister business system if you are building the company from zero or restarting it with focus.

## What UtahLister Is

UtahLister is a done-for-you marketplace listing studio for Utah sellers.

The service turns seller-provided photos and details into a stronger Facebook Marketplace and KSL listing package:

- pricing lane
- ready-to-post copy
- photo order guidance
- buyer reply prompts
- optional guided posting support

The seller keeps the account, the item, and the payout.

## Who It Is For

Start with:

- movers and downsizers
- busy households with quality items ready to sell
- local sellers who already have photos but keep delaying the post

Do not build around:

- personal resale inventory
- bulk flipper operations
- shipping-first ecommerce
- vague coaching or generic AI services

## Current Default Business Decisions

- UtahLister stays a service business, not a software product, for this phase.
- The launch wedge is outdoor gear plus quality furniture and home goods.
- Founder example listings can be used only when they are clearly labeled as example case studies.
- Customer proof should replace founder proof over time, not the other way around.
- Lead automation stays untouched until the repo, site, and Notion system are fully stable.

## First 30-Day Build Sequence

### Week 1

- finalize brand, slogan, and proof-labeling rules
- clean the repo so it reflects customer work only
- finish the homepage copy, trust language, and intake path

### Week 2

- tighten the offer, pricing guardrails, and delivery workflow
- organize proof assets and sample deliverables
- prepare partner-facing and lead-gen messaging

### Week 3

- build the Notion operating layer from the finalized docs
- mirror the approved templates and databases
- verify the source-of-truth order stays clean

### Week 4

- launch outreach and local trust channels
- collect early customer proof and reviews
- keep the one-item offer stable long enough to learn from real sales

## What Good Looks Like

You know this repo is working when a beginner founder can answer these questions quickly:

- Who is the first ideal customer?
- What problem is UtahLister solving?
- What exactly does the client buy?
- How does delivery work from intake to follow-up?
- Why would someone trust this instead of doing it alone?
- How does the website convert a stranger into a lead?
- Where does live operating work happen after the repo is stable?

If any of those answers are still fuzzy, fix the docs before adding more tools, automation, or complexity.
