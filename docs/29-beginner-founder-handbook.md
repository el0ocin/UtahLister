# Beginner Founder Handbook

This is the founder operating guide for someone building UtahLister from a brand-new business stage.

## What You Are Actually Building

You are building a service business, not an app.

The first job is to reliably help real people get better marketplace listings live.

That means your first system needs to support:

- clear positioning
- clean intake
- consistent delivery
- trust-building proof
- local lead flow

## Minimum Business Stack

Before scaling anything, make sure these basics are real:

- domain and live website
- intake form that routes somewhere dependable
- business email
- payment method for service fees
- clear terms and boundaries
- proof storage
- Notion operating layer built from the finalized repo

## What To Sell First

Start with one productized service:

- one item
- one clear price band
- one structured listing package
- one revision round
- one bounded turnaround promise

Do not start with:

- endless custom quoting
- big bundles
- unlimited messaging support
- "we can do anything" positioning

## The Founder Workflow

### Daily

- respond to leads
- clean up intake
- fulfill the current listing queue
- capture proof when outcomes happen

### Weekly

- review the website and form path
- update proof and outreach assets
- track where leads came from
- review what slowed delivery down

### Monthly

- review conversion rates
- adjust pricing only if the workflow data supports it
- promote the best proof into the website and outreach system
- archive outdated research or duplicate assets

## What To Track

Track a short set of business metrics first:

- leads received
- intake completion rate
- average turnaround time
- revision rate
- time from delivery to live post
- item sold or not sold
- proof-worthy wins

## Safety And Boundary Rules

- no passwords
- no MFA codes
- no hidden payout handling
- no exact home addresses in repo or Notion
- no fake claims or fake reviews
- no unlabeled founder proof

These are not optional details. They are part of the brand.

## Beginner Mistakes To Avoid

- trying to serve every seller
- changing the offer before enough data exists
- hiding pricing or process details
- sounding smarter than you sound trustworthy
- treating research as more important than proof and execution
- overbuilding automation before the base workflow is stable

## The Current Default Roadmap

1. Finish the repo and website foundation.
2. Build the Notion operating layer from the approved docs and templates.
3. Turn on local lead generation and partner outreach.
4. Gather customer proof and reviews.
5. Tune automation only after the manual system works cleanly.

This order matters because a messy business system multiplied by automation is still a messy business system.
