# Listing Rescue Service Model

This document defines the secondary UtahLister offer for sellers who already have a live listing but are underpricing the item, presenting it weakly, or letting it sit without a clear rescue plan.

## Short Version

This is not a different company.

It is a sharper entry wedge for the same business.

Instead of leading with:

`Send me your item and I will build the listing from scratch.`

Lead with:

`If your item is live but clearly underperforming, UtahLister can rescue the listing with a better title, pricing lane, category framing, image order, and buyer-handling plan.`

## Why This Offer Exists

The normal UtahLister offer asks the seller to start from zero.

That is useful, but it makes outbound harder because:

- the seller has not taken visible action yet
- the pain is real but not always urgent
- the founder has to explain the service from scratch

The `Listing Rescue` offer fixes that.

It targets people who have already shown:

- intent to sell
- enough motivation to post
- a live listing that can be reviewed
- visible signs that the listing is weak, stale, underpriced, or confusing

That makes outreach easier because the problem is concrete and visible.

## Core Business Model

### What UtahLister sells

UtahLister sells listing rescue and relaunch support for live local marketplace listings.

The seller keeps:

- the account
- the item
- the messages
- the pickup
- the payout

UtahLister provides:

- a rescue diagnosis
- a better pricing lane
- a better title and description
- a stronger category/frame for the item
- a better cover-photo recommendation
- a better gallery order
- missing-detail prompts
- buyer-reply scripts
- optional guided relist support

### What UtahLister is not selling

- guaranteed sale prices
- guaranteed sale windows
- full brokerage
- physical moving help
- direct buyer handling by default
- password-based posting as the normal workflow

## Best Positioning

The strongest positioning is:

`UtahLister helps rescue stale or underperforming Marketplace and KSL listings so better items stop getting undersold or ignored.`

That is stronger than telling strangers:

- they need AI
- they need optimization
- they need a new system

The emotional promise is:

- clarity
- confidence
- less wasted time
- less underpricing
- less guesswork

## Target Audience

### Best-fit sellers

- people with a live listing that has sat too long
- people who dropped the price fast because they got nervous
- people selling bulky or awkward local-pickup furniture
- people with quality items hidden behind weak titles or weak photos
- people who are willing to relist if given a clearer plan

### Best-fit item patterns

- bulky furniture
- dressers, hutches, vanities, cabinets, desks, shelving pieces
- outdoor gear with obvious value but weak presentation
- quality home goods that need dimensions, logistics, or condition clarity

### Worst-fit leads

- junk items with no margin to save
- sellers who want a guaranteed high sale price
- policy-sensitive or risky categories
- listings where the item itself is the main problem, not the listing
- sellers who refuse to provide missing factual details

## The Wedge

The best outreach wedge is not:

`You priced this wrong.`

The best wedge is:

`This looks like a better item than the listing makes it seem.`

That keeps the seller out of defense mode.

Better framing:

- the listing is underselling the item
- the buyer friction is too high
- the item needs stronger framing, not just a lower price

## Lead Identification Rules

Use `Listing Rescue` when at least one of these is visible:

- listing has been live for `14+ days`
- the seller has already made one or more price drops
- title is too generic
- category framing is weak or wrong
- the lead image is weak
- the copy is missing dimensions, transport info, or included-parts clarity
- the item looks stronger than the asking price suggests

Use extra caution when:

- the item looks damaged
- the listing is already very complete and still not moving
- the seller is pricing low on purpose for immediate removal

In those cases the issue may be the item, not the listing.

## Founder Validation Rule

Do not lead with outbound rescue before proving the rescue logic on your own stale listings.

Use your current Facebook Marketplace listings as the first lab:

1. identify the stalled listings
2. run the same rescue process on your own items
3. document what changed
4. measure views, saves, messages, and sales after the rescue

That gives UtahLister proof before you start DMing strangers.

This should be the founder proving ground before broad rescue outreach.

## Outreach Model

### Channel

Phase 1 should focus on:

- manual Facebook Marketplace DMs
- manual KSL outreach only where appropriate

Do not automate this at first.

### Outreach trigger

Reach out only when the listing clearly matches the rescue criteria.

### Message goal

The goal is not to close the whole sale in one message.

The goal is to get permission for a short rescue review.

### Suggested opening angle

Use something close to:

`Hey, I came across your listing because I work on local Marketplace/KSL listing rescue for sellers with good items that are getting undersold or stuck. This looks like a stronger piece than the listing is making it seem. If you want, I can send a few specific ideas that could help it move without just panic-dropping the price.`

That is better than:

- telling them the price is wrong
- sounding like spam
- pitching AI

### Follow-up rule

If there is no reply, do not hammer them.

One follow-up is enough.

If they do not want help, move on.

## Intake Design

## Short answer

Do not create a fully custom intake form for every listing before they respond.

That is too much work too early.

Use a two-step intake:

### Step 1: Rescue screen

Ask for:

- listing URL
- platform
- current price
- city / pickup area
- sale goal: quick sale, max profit, or not sure
- whether they are willing to relist

This is enough to decide whether UtahLister should proceed.

### Step 2: Rescue addendum

Only after they say yes, send a targeted follow-up intake based on the item type.

This should include the normal intake fields plus rescue-specific missing details.

### Why this is better than a fully custom first form

- faster to send
- easier for the seller to complete
- lower outreach friction
- still lets UtahLister collect item-specific details later

## Standard Vs Dynamic Intake

### Base form

Use one standard rescue intake form for every listing.

It should ask for:

- item name
- live listing URL
- current platform(s)
- current asking price
- city / pickup area
- sale goal
- whether they are open to relisting
- current photos or photo folder

### Dynamic add-on questions

Then layer in item-type-specific questions only when needed.

For furniture, the add-on should ask:

- width, depth, and height
- approximate weight if known
- how many people are needed to move it
- whether it can be disassembled
- whether glass shelves, mirrors, hutches, or tops detach
- whether drawers slide properly
- whether there are scratches, chips, stains, or odor issues
- whether the piece is solid wood, veneer, laminate, MDF, or unknown

This is the right middle ground.

A fully custom intake for every listing is too slow.
A fully generic intake is too weak for hard-to-move furniture.

## Pricing Model

### Best structure

Make this a fixed-fee rescue service.

Recommended structure:

- `Micro Rescue`
  One existing listing review plus rewritten title, description, and price lane.
- `Full Rescue`
  Everything in Micro Rescue plus image-order recommendation, missing-detail prompts, buyer replies, and relist support.
- `Rescue + Relist Assist`
  Full Rescue plus a guided posting/relisting session.

### Pricing logic

Keep the pricing low enough to make a stale seller say yes.

You are not selling a big consulting engagement.

You are selling:

- a second chance
- a clearer plan
- a faster decision

Suggested beta logic:

- low-value item rescue: cheap fixed fee
- mid-value item rescue: slightly higher fixed fee
- higher-value or bulky furniture rescue: higher fixed fee only when the value upside is clearly there

### Free tests

Use free rescue only for:

- founder proof
- unusually strong test cases
- outreach experiments where the proof value is higher than the fee

Do not normalize free work.

## Step-By-Step Service Workflow

### Phase 1: find candidate listing

1. find a live listing that looks stale, underpriced, or weakly positioned
2. check that the item looks worth rescuing
3. avoid listings where the item is too low-value or obviously junk

### Phase 2: outreach

4. send one clean rescue DM
5. if the seller replies, ask permission to review and help
6. do not argue if the seller is defensive

### Phase 3: rescue screen

7. collect the short rescue screen
8. decide whether the listing is actually rescuable
9. if yes, move to full intake

### Phase 4: rescue intake

10. send the standard intake plus item-specific add-on questions
11. collect missing details, especially transport/logistics details for furniture
12. confirm whether the seller wants speed or stronger price

### Phase 5: operator review

13. diagnose why the listing is underperforming
14. decide whether the real issue is:
    - price
    - title
    - category
    - photos
    - missing details
    - buyer friction
    - some combination

### Phase 6: rescue package

15. create the rescue package:
    - selected lane
    - revised posted price
    - revised title
    - revised description
    - cover image recommendation
    - gallery order
    - buyer reply prompts
    - relist guidance

### Phase 7: relaunch

16. seller updates or relists the item from their own account
17. UtahLister guides the relaunch if needed
18. listing URL is saved

### Phase 8: follow-up

19. review the listing after 48 hours
20. check whether views or replies improved
21. record the outcome

## What The Application Should Eventually Support

The private UtahLister application should support this offer without becoming a different product.

### Needed rescue workflow support

- import existing listing URL
- classify listing as `new listing build` or `listing rescue`
- store current asking price and rescue notes
- collect rescue-screen fields first
- trigger furniture-specific intake add-ons
- produce a rescue package instead of only a new-listing package
- store before / after title, price, and copy
- track rescue outcomes separately from fresh listings

### Rescue-specific outputs

The app should eventually generate:

- rescue diagnosis summary
- revised pricing lane
- revised title and description
- relist recommendation
- missing-detail checklist
- 48-hour rescue follow-up recommendation

### Not needed yet

Do not build:

- direct scraping automation
- mass outreach automation
- automatic repricing on seller accounts
- autoposting from UtahLister-owned accounts

## Key Metrics

Track these separately from normal UtahLister jobs:

- rescue outreach reply rate
- rescue intake conversion rate
- rescue relist completion rate
- time from rescue package to relist
- view change after rescue
- inquiry change after rescue
- sale rate after rescue
- price recovery vs pre-rescue listing

## Decision Rule

This is worth pursuing if:

- sellers respond positively to the rescue angle
- the work is faster to close than from-scratch jobs
- rescue jobs create strong proof
- UtahLister can clearly improve outcomes on stale listings

This is not worth making primary if:

- sellers resist outside input
- most rescue candidates are low-quality junk
- the item quality is usually too weak for meaningful upside
- the outreach cost is too high relative to conversions

## Current Recommendation

Do not fully pivot the business.

Do this instead:

1. keep the main UtahLister offer
2. add `Listing Rescue` as the early outbound wedge
3. test it first on your own stale listings
4. then test it on a small number of outside listings
5. promote it harder only if the proof says it works

That is the lowest-risk way to learn whether this angle is just easier to message or actually a better business engine.
