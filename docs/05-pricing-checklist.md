# Pricing Checklist

Use this after the operator draft and before posting.

## Quick sale indicators
- broad local demand
- seasonal timing helps right now
- bulky item the seller wants gone
- common household item
- price-sensitive buyer pool
- many local comps

## Max profit indicators
- niche buyer pool
- strong brand or scarcity
- high original retail
- limited local supply
- collector or enthusiast appeal
- the seller can wait

## Lane review steps
1. Compare `Quick Sale Price` and `Max Profit Price` to the private floor if the seller gave one.
2. Make sure the recommended posted price fits the selected lane.
3. Check whether the item needs a fast local move or a patient sale.
4. Check if the item category is seasonal right now.
5. Confirm the first two lines justify the posted price.

## Default pricing rules
- Use clean, believable local pricing
- Prefer prices ending in 5 or 9 when it feels natural
- Do not choose `max profit` if the seller clearly wants speed
- Do not set a posted price so close to the private floor that every negotiation feels painful

## Window guidance
- `quick sale` aims for likely movement in `3-7 days`
- `max profit` aims for likely movement in `14-21 days`
- these are planning targets, not customer guarantees

## 48-hour pricing review
If an item is still live after 48 hours:
- low views: strengthen title and lead line first
- decent views, weak replies: adjust price slightly
- lots of interest: hold firm
- default reduction: 5% to 8%

## Never do this
- invent unrealistic retail anchors
- price off national shipped listings without adjusting for local pickup
- make a huge drop on day one
- bury the best differentiator under filler text

## Final check
Before posting, ask:

`Would a local buyer looking at three similar listings understand why this price makes sense in the first two lines?`
