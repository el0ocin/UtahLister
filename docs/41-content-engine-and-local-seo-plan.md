# Content Engine And Local SEO Plan

This document is the canonical public-safe plan for UtahLister's blog, search, and educational content layer.

## What the content engine is for

The content engine is not a generic "brand awareness" project.

Its job is to:

- capture Utah-specific seller search demand
- explain the two service paths clearly
- build trust before direct contact
- create reusable proof, FAQ, and referral assets
- support local SEO without promising more scale than the business can currently handle

## Core content strategy

The strongest content direction is `problem-first`, not slogan-first.

That means UtahLister should publish around the questions and frustrations sellers actually have:

- why a KSL listing is not getting views
- how to price furniture for Facebook Marketplace
- whether to use KSL or Facebook first
- when to rewrite a listing versus lower the price
- how to photograph bulky items so buyers trust the listing

This content should sound like a practical local operator, not a generic marketing brand.

## Primary content pillars

### 1. Platform choice and mechanics

Use content that helps sellers decide:

- `KSL vs Facebook Marketplace`
- when to cross-post
- how local pickup behavior changes by platform
- platform-specific listing freshness, renewal, and rescue considerations

### 2. Pricing and value framing

Use content that teaches:

- `Quick Sale` versus `Max Profit`
- asking price, target price, and private floor
- 48-hour triage
- bundle versus split decisions
- category-specific pricing logic

### 3. Visual trust and listing quality

Use content that explains:

- why the first photo matters
- why stock photos reduce trust in local resale
- the role of dimensions and `fit proof`
- honest flaw disclosure
- better gallery order and cover-photo choices

### 4. Rescue and relist guidance

Use content that supports `Listing Rescue`:

- stale-listing diagnosis
- low-visibility titles
- weak descriptions
- poor category selection
- price mismatch versus presentation mismatch

### 5. Safety, buyer communication, and local trust

Use content that covers:

- safe meetup habits
- response timing
- lowball handling
- common scam patterns
- how seller communication affects trust

## Priority category hubs

The strongest early content hubs are:

- furniture and bulky items
- outdoor gear
- baby and kids gear
- bikes
- tools

These categories overlap with Utah demand, seller confusion, and UtahLister's current service logic.

Secondary content can later expand into:

- appliances and electronics
- home decor
- seasonal moving and dorm items

## Recommended content formats

Use a narrow set of high-signal formats:

- `How-to` problem-solving posts
- `KSL vs Facebook` comparison posts
- case studies using `Challenge - Approach - Outcome`
- seasonal category roundups
- safety and communication explainers
- partner/referral enablement pieces

Every strong post should be reusable as:

- a social snippet
- a newsletter section
- an FAQ answer
- a proof or trust asset

## 90-day publishing direction

The April 28, 2026 blog research supports a structured first 90 days:

- launch with foundational posts on platform choice, listing visibility, safety, and core mistakes
- follow with pricing and rescue content
- build into visual-trust, category-hub, and case-study content
- end each month with a short synthesis or newsletter-style roundup

The content cadence should be ambitious enough to generate learning, but not so aggressive that it outruns proof or operational capacity.

## CTA mapping

Content should push to the right service path.

Use `New Listing Build` CTAs when the reader:

- has not posted yet
- needs a listing from scratch
- mainly needs clarity and setup

Use `Listing Rescue` CTAs when the reader:

- already has a live listing
- is dealing with poor views or no messages
- needs a relist or diagnosis

Do not use generic "contact us to learn more" language if a narrower CTA is possible.

## Editorial rules

Keep the content readable and operationally honest:

- use short paragraphs, usually `3-5` sentences
- use descriptive `H3` subheads
- use local modifiers such as `Utah`, `Salt Lake`, `Utah County`, or `Wasatch Front` when natural
- use screenshots, before/after examples, and proof only when they are truthfully labeled
- end with one clear CTA

Avoid:

- hype language
- miracle claims
- guaranteed-outcome wording
- content that sounds like generic "AI optimization"

## What this means for UtahLister

The content engine should become:

- a trust layer for the website
- a slow-burn acquisition channel
- a partner credibility asset
- a structured place to store repeatable selling education

It should not become:

- a content treadmill disconnected from service delivery
- a replacement for proof
- an excuse to delay live intake verification

## Success signals

Judge the content engine by:

- qualified organic traffic
- stronger search relevance around Utah-specific seller problems
- intake submissions tied to content
- partner usefulness
- proof assets created from case studies and educational breakdowns

If content attracts attention but not good-fit leads, adjust the topics and CTAs instead of simply publishing more.
