# Founder Example Case Study: Trek Marlin 5 Repost Win

This is the flagship founder-run example proof asset for `UtahLister`.

Use it as an example case study only.

Do not present this file as customer proof.

## Snapshot
- Item: Trek Marlin 5 Mountain Bike `(L 29 CH), 2022-2023`
- Area: Lehi, Utah
- Platforms used: Facebook Marketplace and KSL
- Original asking price: `$600`
- Revised asking price: `$475`
- Selected lane: `quick sale`
- Original listing date: `March 18, 2026`
- Revised Facebook listing date: `March 24, 2026`
- Sold date: `March 25, 2026`

## Starting problem
Based on your notes:
- the bike sat for about 7 days at `$600`
- Facebook Marketplace had very low traction and no outreach
- KSL had some views, but no meaningful buyer outreach
- the first photo set was clean but too flat and too indoor-looking for a bike sale

## What changed on the revised version
- moved to a faster sale lane with a revised posted price of `$475`
- switched to a stronger outdoor rock-wall cover photo
- used a mixed indoor and outdoor photo set to show different angles and details
- included an unopened chain cleaner to increase perceived value
- used firmer, question-led buyer replies instead of dead-end statements

## Reported outcome
You reported that the revised Facebook Marketplace listing sold on `March 25, 2026` for the full `$475`.

One buyer opened with a `$380` offer, was declined, and still converted at the full price.

## What this proves
- a better environment and stronger cover image can materially improve trust and click-through
- moving to the right lane matters more than defending an inflated first-pass price
- question-led replies can keep the conversation moving without sounding pushy
- a stale listing can turn quickly once photos, pricing, and buyer handling all line up
